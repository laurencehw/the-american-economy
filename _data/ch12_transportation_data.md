## 7. GEOGRAPHIC PATTERNS

**Major Freight Corridors:**
- I-80 & I-90: Primary East-West truck corridors
- I-5 & I-95: Primary coastal North-South corridors

**Intermodal Hubs:**
- **Chicago:** Nexus of US rail network; all Class I railroads meet here
- **Inland Empire (SoCal):** "Warehouse of America" for LA/Long Beach imports
- **Memphis:** FedEx global hub ("Aerotropolis") and rail/truck crossroads

---

## 8. REGULATION

**Key Agencies:**
- DOT: Dept. of Transportation (overarching)
- FMCSA: Trucking safety (Hours-of-Service: 11-hour driving limit in 14-hour window)
- FRA: Rail safety
- STB: Surface Transportation Board (rail economic regulation)
- FAA: Aviation

**Deregulation History:**
- Airlines (1978): Ended price setting, created hub-and-spoke
- Trucking (1980): Motor Carrier Act ended route/rate controls
- Rail (1980): Staggers Act allowed market rates, line abandonment

---

## 9. RECENT TRENDS

1. **E-commerce Transformation:** "Push" (bulk to store) → "Pull" (parcel to door)
2. **Last-Mile Competition:** Amazon delivers more packages than UPS or FedEx
3. **Supply Chain Bullwhip:** 2021-22 disruption → inventory buildup → soft 2023 demand
4. **Autonomous Trucking:** Pilots active in Southwest (Aurora, Kodiak Robotics)
5. **Amazon Logistics Empire:** 27% of US parcel volume (surpassing UPS at 21%, FedEx at 15%)
