## 1. MANUFACTURING SIZE

- **GDP Contribution (2023):** $2.3 Trillion (Chained 2017 Dollars)
- **Share of Total GDP:** 10.2%
- **Employment (Current):** ~12.8 Million (approx. 8% of workforce)
- **Employment Trend:**
  - 2000: 17.3 Million (Peak)
  - 2010: 11.5 Million (Post-recession Low)
  - 2023/24: 12.8 Million (Flat/Declining recently)
- **The Divergence:** Since 2000, manufacturing employment has **dropped by ~26%**, while real output has **risen by >20%**. Factories produce more value with fewer workers.

## 4. GEOGRAPHIC PATTERNS

### The "Manufacturing Belt" (Midwest)
- States: MI, OH, IN, WI, IL
- Status: Retains ~30% of manufacturing jobs, shifting to EV batteries
- Examples: GM Ultium plants in MI/OH, Intel fab in Ohio

### The "Battery Belt" (Southern Growth)
- States: GA, TN, KY, SC, AL
- Drivers: Right-to-Work laws, lower energy/labor costs, logistics
- Key Sites: Hyundai (AL/GA), BMW (SC), BlueOval City (TN), SK Battery (GA)

### Aerospace Clusters
- Commercial: Seattle/Everett, WA (Boeing); Charleston, SC
- Defense: Southern CA, Huntsville (AL), Dallas-Fort Worth (TX)

### Pharma/Bio Clusters
- Northeast: New Jersey ("The Medicine Chest"), Boston (Biotech)
- Mid-Atlantic: NC Research Triangle

### Tech Hardware
- Silicon Valley (CA), "Silicon Desert" (AZ - Intel/TSMC), "Silicon Forest" (OR)

## 6. TRADE AND SUPPLY CHAINS

- **Goods Trade Deficit (2023):** $1.06 Trillion
- **Strategic Investments:**
  - CHIPS Act: $39B in grants → $200B+ private semiconductor investment
  - IRA: $86B+ in clean energy manufacturing projects (Year 1)
- **Construction Boom:** Manufacturing construction spending doubled (2021–2024), reaching ~$225B annual rate

## Supply Chain Review (Dec 2024) Key Points

From the Quadrennial Supply Chain Review:

- Critical sectors: Energy, batteries, critical minerals, agri-food, pharma, ICT, semiconductors, transportation, defense
- $1 trillion+ in announced private investment since 2021
- $796 billion specifically in new American manufacturing
- CHIPS Act: $53B federal investment → $446B announced private investment
- 700,000 manufacturing jobs added since Jan 2021
- Key vulnerabilities: China dependence, climate risks, cyber threats
